        <h1>Mail Online becomes world's second most popular newspaper site</h1>
        <p id="stand-first" class="stand-first-alone">Associated Newspapers' online network passes Huffington Post with 39.6m unique visitors in March</p>
    </div>
</div>

                         <div id="content"><ul class="article-attributes"><li class="byline"> <a class="contributor" href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/profile/josh-halliday"> Josh Halliday</a> </li> <li class="publication"> <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/">guardian.co.uk</a>, <time datetime="2011-04-19T11:36BST">Tuesday 19 April 2011 11.36 BST</time> </li> </ul> 
<div id="article-wrapper" data-global-auto-refresh-switch="on">
<div id="main-content-picture"><img src="http://static.guim.co.uk/sys-images/Media/Pix/pictures/2011/4/19/1303209301172/Mail-Online-19-April-2011-007.jpg" width="460" height="276" alt="Mail Online 19 April 2011"/> <div class="caption">Mail Online is now only second to the New York Times website</div></div>
<div id="article-body-blocks"><p>Mail Online, Associated <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/newspapers" title="More from guardian.co.uk on Newspapers">Newspapers</a>' online network, has overtaken the <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/huffington-post" title="More from guardian.co.uk on Huffington Post">Huffington Post</a> to become the second most popular news website in the world in March, according to new figures from metrics firm comScore.</p><p/><p>Arianna Huffington's groundbreaking news and opinion website, which was <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/richard-adams-blog/2011/feb/07/huffington-post-sale-aol-ariana" title="bought by AOL for $315m (193m)">bought by AOL for $315m (&#163;193m)</a> in February, was leapfrogged by Mail Online, which is now second only to the <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/new-york-times" title="More from guardian.co.uk on New York Times">New York Times</a> in ComScore's "newspapers" category.</p><p/><p>Mail Online has grown exponentially in the past three years, in part due to its showbiz-laden front page, but also because of its focus on US news. Associated has been hiring editorial staff in New York and Los Angeles to feed its online news operation.</p><p/><p>According to comScore, Mail Online reported a 27% rise in unique visitors between February and March, to 39,635,000. Huffington Post, meanwhile, recorded a 20% lift, to 38,429,000 unique visitors.</p><p/><p>Mail Online remains the most popular UK newspaper website. However, the site suffered <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/2011/mar/24/february-abce-mail-online" title="a slight drop in UK online traffic">a slight drop in UK online traffic</a> in February &#8211; the first in a period of sustained growth, according to the latest figures from the Audit Bureau of Circulations Electronic.</p><p/><p>The New York Times was still by some distance the most popular English-language news site in March. The market-leading title benefited from a remarkable month in world news, reporting a 41% rise in traffic on February to a record 61,964,000 unique visitors.</p><p/><p>However, that figure is expected to drop after the New York Times introduced <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/2011/mar/17/new-york-times-charging" title="unveiled its long-delayed metered subscribtion service">its long-delayed metered subscription service</a> on 28 March, although NYT executives have been careful to only charge the heaviest users of the site.</p><p/><p>There will be three charging options for four weeks' access, with the cheapest at $15 for the NYT website and its smartphone app; $20 for the website and the tablet app; and $35 for all devices.</p><p/><p>The Tribune Newspapers network of websites were the fourth most popular for March, with 34,624,000 unique visitors, the comScore figures reveal.</p><p/><p>Guardian.co.uk, which is part of the group that publishes MediaGuardian.co.uk, was the fifth most read English-language news site in the world, with 30,891,000 users. In March, <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/theguardian" title="More from guardian.co.uk on The Guardian">the Guardian</a> unveiled plans for an expanded digital operation in the US, which will be <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/2011/apr/01/janine-gibson-gnm-us" title="headed up by Guardian.co.uk editor Janine Gibson">headed up by Guardian.co.uk editor Janine Gibson</a>.</p><p/><p>The Guardian has an US online audience of about 8 million users, according to comScore metrics from February 2011.</p><p/><p><em>&#8226; To contact the MediaGuardian news desk email editor@mediaguardian.co.uk or phone 020 3353 3857. For all other inquiries please call the main Guardian switchboard on 020 3353 2000. If you are writing a comment for publication, please mark clearly "for publication".</em></p><p><em>&#8226; To get the latest media news to your desktop or mobile, follow MediaGuardian on </em><a href="http://twitter.com/#!/mediaguardian" title="Twitter"><em>Twitter</em></a><em> and </em><a href="http://www.facebook.com/mediaguardian" title="Facebook"><em>Facebook</em></a><em> </em></p></div>

        <h1>Royal wedding 2.0: Palace gears up for big day</h1>
        <p id="stand-first" class="stand-first-alone">Wedding of Prince William and Kate Middleton will be screened on official YouTube channel with pics posted on Flickr</p>
    </div>
</div>

                         <div id="content"><ul class="article-attributes"><li class="byline"> <a class="contributor" href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/profile/marksweney"> Mark Sweney</a> </li> <li class="publication"> <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/">guardian.co.uk</a>, <time datetime="2011-04-19T16:52BST">Tuesday 19 April 2011 16.52 BST</time> </li> </ul> 
<div id="article-wrapper" data-global-auto-refresh-switch="on">
<div id="main-content-picture"><img src="http://static.guim.co.uk/sys-images/Media/Pix/pictures/2011/4/19/1303228081881/Royal-Wedding-007.jpg" width="460" height="276" alt="Royal Wedding"/> <div class="caption">Prince William and Kate Middleton will drive through Horse Guard Parade after getting married. Photograph: Kerim Okten/EPA</div></div>
<div id="article-body-blocks"><p>The marriage of Prince William and Kate Middleton will be streamed live via YouTube, with royal household staff tweeting updates and posting pictures on photo-sharing site <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/technology/flickr" title="More from guardian.co.uk on Flickr">Flickr</a>.</p><p>Royal household officials on Tuesday unveiled an <a href="http://www.youtube.com/theroyalchannel" title="official YouTube channel">official YouTube channel</a> that will live stream the 29 April event so that <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/technology/internet" title="More from guardian.co.uk on Internet">internet</a> users can watch it anywhere in the world.</p><p/><p><a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/uk/royal-wedding" title="More from guardian.co.uk on Royal wedding">Royal wedding</a> viewers on Google-owned YouTube will get the <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/bbc" title="More from guardian.co.uk on BBC">BBC</a>'s coverage, but without the commentary provided by Huw Edwards and the rest of the corporation's presenting team.</p><p/><p>As well as being broadcast live online royal officials will manage a live multimedia blog and augment coverage on a dedicated Twitter feed, @ClarenceHouse, as well as loading images on to Flickr.</p><p/><p>"Staff at Clarence House and St James's Palace will provide a live commentary with historical information, interesting links, additional photographs and video footage as well as an integrated Twitter feed," the palace said in a statement.</p><p/><p>The royal household is encouraging technologically inclined viewers to interact on the day sending messages of congratulations via a Twitter hash tag, #rw2011, and launching an official YouTube "wedding book" where anyone can submit a video of congratulations.</p><p/><p>"While millions will be in London for the big day, it's clear that people around the world have wedding fever," <a href="http://youtube-global.blogspot.com/2011/04/royal-wedding-live-on-youtube.html" title="said a spokesman for YouTube in a blog post">YouTube announced in a blog post</a>. "Google search trends show that in addition to the UK and the US, the top 10 countries searching for 'royal wedding' include places like Singapore and the Philippines. In response, we've been working to make as much of the big day as possible accessible to everyone."</p><p/><p>For those in different time zones that may find watching live online coverage impossible, YouTube said it will replay the footage immediately after the event concludes. It will then be available to view anytime after.</p><p/><p>Facebook users can also click an "I'm attending" button on the <a href="http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/socialnetworking" title="More from guardian.co.uk on Social networking">social networking</a> service's The British Monarchy page to show their interest.</p><p><em>&#8226; To contact the MediaGuardian news desk email editor@mediaguardian.co.uk or phone 020 3353 3857. For all other inquiries please call the main Guardian switchboard on 020 3353 2000. If you are writing a comment for publication, please mark clearly "for publication".</em></p><p><em>&#8226; To get the latest media news to your desktop or mobile, follow MediaGuardian on </em><a href="http://twitter.com/#!/mediaguardian" title="Twitter"><em>Twitter</em></a><em> at http://twitter.com/#!/mediaguardian and Facebook at http://www.facebook.com/mediaguardian</em></p></div>
